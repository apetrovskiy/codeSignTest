CA RWA #1 - Review Changes - Instructions

 

As a Chief Software Architect (CA), you’ll spend about 25% of your week reviewing change requests and deciding which ones to approve, and which ones to reject. As the gatekeeper for product quality, you have the final word on a product that you know inside and out, so it’s critical that these decisions are made correctly.

 

High-quality change request decisions prevent code and infrastructure changes that don’t accomplish what’s intended or break other functionality, reduce performance, or strain resources. You should also consider that all code being delivered to the product must be of high quality and also highly maintainable. With that in mind, consider the correct usage of industry-proven models and software development methodologies during your review. Although this is not a coaching role, we expect CAs to provide clear decision reasons that will help submitters improve in the future.

 

In this Real Work Assignment (RWA), you will review four real change requests that our CAs reviewed. For each, decide whether to approve or reject the change, and provide concise rationale for any rejections. You may also be asked to identify which architecture artifacts need to be updated, and how.

 

Before you begin, review the materials below. We suggest you open the links in new tabs for reference during your test.
